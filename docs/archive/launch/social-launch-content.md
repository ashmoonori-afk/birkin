## Hacker News — Show HN Post

### Title
Show HN: Birkin – Open-source AI agent platform with self-improving agents and messaging-first design

### Body
Hi HN,

I've been building Birkin, an open-source AI agent platform designed to make powerful AI accessible beyond the developer community.

**The problem:** Every agent framework (AutoGPT, CrewAI, LangChain Agents) assumes Python proficiency, Docker knowledge, and comfort with CLIs. The people who would benefit most from AI agents — small business owners, office workers, freelancers — are locked out.

**What Birkin does differently:**

- **Self-improving agents**: Built-in learning loop that creates skills from experience. Your agent gets better the more you use it, with no manual training.

- **Messaging-first**: Works from Telegram, Discord, Slack, WhatsApp, or Signal. Your agent lives where you already communicate.

- **200+ model providers**: OpenAI, Anthropic, open-source models via Ollama — zero lock-in. Switch with one command.

- **Serverless-ready**: Deploy on Modal for near-zero cost when idle. Or self-host on a $5 VPS.

**Technical details for the curious:**

- Ground-up architecture — 40+ tools, MCP integration, subagent delegation
- Full RL training pipeline for research use
- Python 3.11+, async throughout
- Extensible skill system with community marketplace
- Gateway architecture for multi-platform messaging

**What we're NOT doing:**

- No "magic AI that replaces your job" claims
- No mandatory cloud service — self-host everything
- No vendor lock-in to any model provider
- No enterprise upsell pressure — the free tier is genuinely complete

The premium tier exists for teams that need cybersecurity management, RBAC, and managed hosting. The open-source core has no artificial limitations.

GitHub: https://github.com/MoonGwanghoon/birkin
Docs: https://getbirkin.com

Would love feedback on the developer experience and what use cases you'd want to see first.
