## Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Accessible** | Explain clearly, assume no prior AI knowledge | Dumbed-down, patronizing, oversimplified |
| **Confident** | We believe in what we're building | Arrogant, dismissive of alternatives |
| **Human** | Lead with benefits, back with specs when needed | Jargon-first, spec-sheet marketing |
| **Korean-global** | Built in Korea, designed for the world | Localized only, or ignoring our origin |

### Tone Spectrum

- **Documentation:** Clear, direct, technical when necessary. Use second person ("you"). Keep sentences short.
- **Social media:** Conversational, energetic but not breathless. Show, don't just tell.
- **Blog posts:** Storytelling-first. Open with a human problem, show how Birkin solves it.
- **Error messages:** Helpful, never blaming. Tell users what happened and what to do next.
- **Community replies:** Warm, specific, grateful. Acknowledge the person before addressing the issue.

## Writing Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Use "you" and "your" — talk directly to the reader
- Lead with the benefit, follow with the how
- Use concrete examples over abstract descriptions
- Say "Birkin" not "our platform" or "the system"
- Use present tense for features that exist now

### Don't

- Don't say "revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," or "next-generation"
- Don't assume the reader knows what LLMs, tokens, prompts, or APIs are (in user-facing content)
- Don't use "simply" or "just" before instructions — if it were simple, they wouldn't need the instructions
- Don't frame Birkin as a fork or derivative of another project — it is built from scratch
- Don't default to dark mode in visual assets — use the Birkin Craft warm cream light mode as primary
- Don't use "AI" as a standalone noun ("an AI") — use "AI agent," "AI assistant," or "Birkin"

## Audience Segments

### Non-technical users (primary)

**What they care about:** Does it work? Is it easy? Will it save me time?

**Language:** No jargon. Describe capabilities in terms of outcomes ("manage your emails" not "IMAP integration"). Use analogies to familiar concepts ("like having a capable assistant").

**Example:**
> "Tell Birkin to check your inbox every morning and summarize what needs your attention. It remembers how you like your summaries and gets better at it each time."

### Developers (secondary)

**What they care about:** Architecture, extensibility, model flexibility, self-hosting.

**Language:** Technical specifics welcome. API details, configuration options, architecture decisions.

**Example:**
> "Birkin uses an OpenAI-compatible API abstraction layer with 200+ providers via OpenRouter. Switch models with a single config change. The tool registry is self-registering — add a new tool file and import it."

### Business buyers (tertiary)

**What they care about:** Security, compliance, team management, ROI.

**Language:** Business outcomes, risk mitigation, team-level benefits.

**Example:**
> "Birkin's premium tier adds automated vulnerability scanning, role-based access control, and audit logging. Deploy agents for your team without exposing sensitive data to third-party cloud services."

## Competitor Positioning

When comparing to competitors, be factual and respectful:

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Unlike X, Birkin works from messaging apps you already use" | "X is terrible because it's CLI-only" |
| "Birkin is MIT-licensed — no vendor lock-in" | "Other tools trap you in their ecosystem" |
| "Birkin is designed for non-technical users" | "Other tools are too complicated for normal people" |
